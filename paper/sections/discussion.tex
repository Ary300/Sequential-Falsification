\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
\vspace{-0.4em}
\enlargethispage{8\baselineskip}

\textbf{Empirical results.} Three predictions, three matches. CT1: powered $n\!=\!210$ separates BayesArbiter from AdaCAD/CoCoA/Astute/Self-RAG at $+0.064/+0.043/+0.032/+0.022$, every CI above zero. CT2: $\widehat\rho^\star$ predicts saturation at pooled $r\!=\!0.997$ across $12$ cells / $3$ families. CT3: on the RLCR-trained $8$B worst cell, stacking $\eta$-tempering reaches accuracy $0.531$, ECE $0.431$, Brier $0.415$ (from $0.886$); the matched-base GRPO-vs-DPO gap of $+0.344$ at fixed Llama-8B base gives causal evidence the failure mode is RL-induced, with Phi-3, Mistral, and Gemma reproducing.

\textbf{Hallucination as confidently-wrong output.}  On R1-14B / ConflictBank at $K\!=\!1024$, the model places $96\%$ of its mass on the wrong answer. A single held-out $\eta^\star$ reduces Brier by $44\%$ and quadruples accuracy on the load-bearing slice; operationally this is calibrated temperature scaling~\citep{guo2017oncalibration}, with the safe-Bayes derivation telling the practitioner when $\eta^\star\!<\!1$ applies.

\textbf{Limitations and future work.} Causal anchor sits on Llama lineage + Phi-3 / Mistral / Gemma cross-family; a fourth-base contrast is open. CT2 is tight on the stable-answer subset and misses on Qwen2.5-32B / ConflictBank where an absolute-confidence floor sits outside the linear regime (App.~\ref{app:limitations}). Sequence-level $\eta$ across multi-token responses, $K\!=\!3$ multi-source on real corpora, and frontier-API end-to-end deployment are next; long-horizon agentic chains (\Cref{thm:t15-compositional}), causal identification of $w^\star$ on logged traffic (\Cref{thm:t16-causal}), and the Stackelberg adversary inside CT1$'$ are the three open directions.
