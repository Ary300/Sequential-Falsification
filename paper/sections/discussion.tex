\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
\vspace{-0.4em}

\textbf{What the theory predicts and what the data shows.} CT1 says the Bayes-optimal arbitrator is the geometric mixture with weight $w^\star\!=\!\E[r\!\mid\!s]$ on $\log p_\ctx$, and that every published decoder sits at a fixed point on the same exponent axis (\Cref{cor:special-cases-body}). The data shows what the corollary predicts: BayesArbiter beats every named baseline on $23/25$ conflict cells with e-value $2806$, and powered head-to-heads against three tuned adaptive baselines clear zero at $+0.043 / +0.032 / +0.022$ in regret. CT2 says the chain-of-thought iterate converges at a rate $\rho^\star$ that lives inside an $\eta$-linear sandwich. The data shows a ridge-LS estimate $\widehat\rho^\star$ predicting empirical $\TV(p_t,p_K)$ decay within $\pm 0.07$ across $12$ cells over $3$ families ($r\!=\!0.997$). CT3 says the Brier improvement under $\eta$-tempering is closed-form and equals $(1\!-\!\eta^\star)^{2}V_{\mathrm{post}}$ to leading order. The data shows a held-out tempering scalar moving the worst-cell readout from accuracy $0.037$ to $0.440$ and Brier $0.903$ to $0.505$; stacked with RLCR, the same slice reaches accuracy $0.531$ and ECE $0.431$. The matched-base RL contrast at fixed Llama-3.1-8B base separates conflict-slice ECE by $+0.344$ between DPO and GRPO, with the Llama lineage and a Phi-3 cross-family replication reproducing the direction. Three predictions, three matches, one decoder line.

\textbf{Hallucination as confidently-wrong output.}  On R1-14B / ConflictBank at $K\!=\!1024$, the model places $96\%$ of its mass on the wrong answer. The closed-form CT3 readout corrects this without retraining: a single $\eta^\star$ scalar selected on a held-out objective reduces Brier by $44\%$ and quadruples accuracy on the load-bearing slice. Operationally, this is calibrated temperature scaling~\citep{guo2017oncalibration}; the safe-Bayes derivation is what tells the practitioner when $\eta^\star\!<\!1$ is the right call.

\textbf{Limitations.} Three boundaries the present account does not cross. The matched-base causal claim is anchored on the Llama lineage with a Phi-3 cross-family replication; isolating the same DPO-clean / GRPO-bad split on a third base is open. The CT2 trajectory inequality is tight inside the stable-answer subset; it misses on Qwen2.5-32B with ConflictBank, where an absolute-confidence floor lives outside the linear-contraction regime the rate envelope covers (App.~\ref{app:limitations}). The closed-candidate scope of CT1 is not a hard ceiling at $n\!\approx\!200$ free-form decoding (Bayes ties AdaCAD on EM, beats CAD; App.~\ref{app:freeform-openqa}), but composing the trace term $p_K^\eta$ across multi-token responses requires a sequence-level extension that is future work. Multi-source $K\!=\!3$ composition is shown on synthetic data; all experiments are in English; the frontier validation uses top-$k$ logprobs rather than end-to-end deployment on closed-weight APIs.

\textbf{Future work.} The free-energy view points at three directions where reliability is the load-bearing latent. \emph{Long-horizon agentic chains}, where each tool call plays the role of a retrieval pass and the trace dynamics CT2 describes compose multiplicatively (\Cref{thm:t15-compositional}); the same Gibbs minimizer with a $K$-hop product of context terms gives a candidate decoder for tool-using agents. \emph{Causal identification of trust at scale}, where $w^\star$ is the doubly-robust ATE of conditioning on the retrieved context (\Cref{thm:t16-causal}); estimating it from logged production traffic gives a practical recipe for auditing deployed RAG stacks without controlled experiments. \emph{Adversarial robustness}, where the four-channel CT1$'$ envelope already absorbs TV-bounded poisoning of the retrieval distribution; tightening the lower bound on the worst-case adversary moves the discussion from upper-bound robustness to a Stackelberg game whose equilibrium is the saddle point of $\mathcal{F}$. The pattern is the same in each direction: once context reliability is the parameter you estimate, hallucination, calibration, and adversarial behavior become predictable rather than emergent. The closed-form scalar in CT3 tells the practitioner what to do at decode time, and the spectral diagnostic in CT2 tells them whether their trace is in a regime where reasoning helps or hurts. Both interventions ship as one inference-time line over a standard RAG decoder.
