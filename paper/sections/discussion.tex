\paragraph{Conclusion.}
We treat arbitration as parameter estimation on a single free-energy functional and recover the combiner, the trajectory envelope, and the deployment scalar from one object. CT1 pins the unique reliability-weighted geometric mixture $\hat p_{w^\star}\!\propto\!p_\theta^{1-w^\star}p_\ctx^{w^\star}$; CAD, AdaCAD, CoCoA, Astute, Self-RAG, JuICE collapse to special cases on its exponent axis. CT2 makes the chain-of-thought rate $\widehat\rho^\star$ observable. CT3 gives the closed-form Brier improvement $(1\!-\!\eta^\star)^{2}V_{\mathrm{post}}$ at decode time. Three predictions, three matches: powered $n\!=\!210$ separates BayesArbiter from AdaCAD/CoCoA/Astute/Self-RAG at $+0.064/+0.043/+0.032/+0.022$ with every CI above zero; $\widehat\rho^\star$ predicts saturation at pooled $r\!=\!0.997$; on the RLCR-trained $8$B worst cell, $\eta$-tempering moves accuracy $0.063\!\to\!0.531$ and Brier $0.886\!\to\!0.415$ with no gradient step; the matched-base GRPO-vs-DPO gap of $+0.344$, replicated across five families, gives the first causal evidence a specific post-training recipe induces context-overtrust. Hallucination, conflict-slice calibration, and the long-CoT-helps-or-hurts disagreement reduce to one Bayesian estimation problem on one functional, and the same functional is the natural substrate for tool-using agents, multi-hop retrieval, and adversarial robustness: every voice a future LLM listens to becomes a reliability variable to estimate, not a heuristic to tune.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The causal anchor for the matched-base contrast sits on Llama lineage with a Phi-3, Mistral, and Gemma cross-family replication, and a fourth from-scratch base would harden the claim that GRPO induces context-overtrust as a recipe-level property rather than a Llama artefact. CT2's spectral diagnostic is tight on the stable-answer subset and predicts decay within $\pm 0.07$ across twelve cells, but the Qwen2.5-32B and ConflictBank slices sit in an absolute-confidence floor regime that lives outside the linear sandwich, where the rate underpredicts overshoot (App.~\ref{app:limitations}). CT1's closed-candidate setup is not a hard ceiling at the $n\!\approx\!200$ free-form scale we tested (BayesArbiter ties AdaCAD on EM and beats CAD), but composing $p_K^\eta$ across multi-token responses needs a sequence-level extension we sketch but do not run end-to-end. The multi-source $K\!=\!3$ result is currently synthetic; every experiment is in English; and frontier validation goes through top-$k$ logprobs because we do not have weight access to GPT-4o, Claude, or Gemini. None of these is a refutation of the theory; each is a place where the next round of evidence has to land.

\paragraph{Future work.}
Three concrete directions follow from the same functional. First, long-horizon agentic chains: tool calls compose multiplicatively (\Cref{thm:t15-compositional}), and the same Gibbs minimizer with a $K$-hop product of context terms is the candidate decoder for agents mixing browsing, code execution, and planning. Second, causal identification of $w^\star$ at scale via the doubly-robust ATE of context-conditioning (\Cref{thm:t16-causal}), estimable from logged production traffic without a controlled experiment. Third, adversarial robustness via the Stackelberg equilibrium of $\mathcal{F}$ inside CT1$'$, opening a route to provably poison-resistant decoders. Beyond these, the horizon is a single estimator that runs across every voice a frontier LLM listens to --- retrieval, tools, planners, sub-agents, and the model's own thought --- with one functional governing every arbitration. Reliability stops being the residual a deployment engineer hand-tunes and becomes the latent the model estimates end-to-end in the same forward pass it uses to answer.
