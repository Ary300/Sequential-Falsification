\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A 2023-trained language model knows Elon Musk runs Twitter. But a 2024 article retrieved by RAG names Linda Yaccarino as CEO of X. How is the model supposed to choose?

Such conflict is the rule, not the exception, in retrieval-augmented systems. The model carries a parametric prior $p_\theta(y\mid q)$ from training, conditions on a retrieved context $p_{\ctx}(y\mid q,c)$, and, if it produces a chain of thought, listens to itself across $K$ reasoning steps before answering. Any of those voices can be wrong, and they often disagree. Whatever rule the system uses to arbitrate fires on every query and silently fixes hallucination rate, calibration on conflict slices, and worst-case behavior under poisoned or stale retrieval.

The rule is silent in deployment. It picks a source and ships the answer; the conflict never surfaces, and the user has no signal that a choice was made. Arbitration is the chokepoint where training-cutoff lag and retrieval drift collapse into a single decision per query. A medical assistant trained before a label change either reads the updated dose off the chart or quietly defaults to the older one. Both choices ship; only one is right. The same scenario plays out on legal precedent, financial disclosures, and any domain where the world updates faster than the next pretraining run.

Today that rule is set by hand. Production RAG systems trust retrieval unconditionally, trust the parametric model unconditionally, or interpolate with tuned thresholds and learned controllers~\citep{mallen2023popqa,asai2024selfrag,yan2024crag}. Decoder-level methods like Context-Aware Decoding~\citep{shi2024cad} and its variants AdaCAD~\citep{wang2025adacad} and CoCoA~\citep{khandelwal2025cocoa} sharpen the heuristic but stay heuristic. The calibration literature on reasoning models has split into camps that look mutually contradictory: longer chain-of-thought helps calibration on standard QA~\citep{yoon2025confidence} and hurts it on harder or misspecified tasks~\citep{halawi2024overthinking,damani2025rlcr}.

None of these approaches is derived from a posterior over how reliable the context actually is. CAD and AdaCAD weight the context-conditional against the prior with fixed or first-order-corrected exponents the authors do not interpret as a Bayes-optimal action; CoCoA averages reliability proxies without a consistency guarantee. Survey work~\citep{xu2024knowledgeconflicts} flags that fixed trust in either source is suboptimal and stops there. The calibration tension shares this root cause: no theory tells the practitioner which regime they are in, so the same chain-of-thought scaling argument supports both ``reasoning helps calibration'' and ``reasoning hurts calibration'' depending on which subset of the literature is consulted.

We give a principled account. \textsc{BayesArbiter} (\Cref{fig:architecture}) treats arbitration as parameter estimation. The latent variable is context reliability $r\!\in\![0,1]$, with prior $\pi(r)$ informed by observable signals such as BM25, entity popularity, and semantic entropy. Four classical axioms pin the unique combiner to the geometric mixture $p_\theta^{1-w^\star}p_{\ctx}^{w^\star}$ with $w^\star\!=\!\E[r\mid q,c]$. CAD, AdaCAD, and CoCoA fall out as approximations of this rule. For the chain of thought, the trace itself reads as endogenous generalized-Bayes evidence, and the local convergence rate at the fixed point ties an empirically observable spectral diagnostic on logged trajectories to the per-cell saturation slope. When the post-trace distribution overshoots, inference-time tempering ($\eta$-Gibbs at readout) reverses it, with a closed-form Brier improvement. Both interventions ship at inference time over a standard RAG decoder.

\textsc{BayesArbiter} clears zero against $7$ of $10$ named baselines on the $25$-cell spotlight, including a $+0.083$ advantage over the heuristic-adaptive primary on $23/25$ cells (e-value $2806$, CI $[0.037,0.111]$); the four tightest competitors (AdaCAD, CoCoA, Astute~RAG, Self-RAG) clear zero on the powered $n\!=\!210$ matched design at $+0.064 / +0.043 / +0.032 / +0.022$, AdaCAD's CI $[0.060,0.068]$ the tightest of the four. The trajectory diagnostic predicts trace decay within $\pm 0.07$ across $12$ cells over three model families ($r\!=\!0.997$). On the load-bearing RLCR-trained $8$B worst cell (ConflictBank, $K\!=\!1024$), $\eta$-tempering moves accuracy from $0.063$ to $0.531$, ECE from $0.904$ to $0.431$, and Brier from $0.886$ to $0.415$; on a standalone R1-14B cell at the same benchmark, $\eta$-tempering alone drops Brier from $0.903$ to $0.505$. A matched-base RL contrast at a fixed Llama-3.1-8B base separates conflict-slice ECE by $+0.344$ between DPO and GRPO; the Llama lineage and a cross-family Phi-3 replication reproduce the direction.

Our contribution is three theorems on a single free-energy functional $\mathcal{F}$, two inference-time interventions, and a unification of the existing decoder literature.

\textbf{CT1, Bayes-optimal arbitration (\Cref{thm:ct1-body}).} The geometric mixture $p \propto p_\theta^{1-w}p_\ctx^{w}p_K^{\eta}$ with $w^\star\!=\!\E[r\!\mid\!s]$ is the unique Gibbs minimizer of $\mathcal{F}[p]\!=\!\E_p[E_{w,\eta}]\!-\!H[p]$ and the unique combiner under four classical axioms. CAD, AdaCAD, CoCoA, Astute, Self-RAG, and JuICE all collapse to special cases on this exponent axis (\Cref{cor:special-cases-body}). A four-channel TV-perturbation envelope absorbs model error, signal noise, finite-sample variance, and adversarial poisoning into one regret inequality.

\textbf{CT2, trajectory envelope on $\mathcal{F}$ (\Cref{thm:ct2-body}).} The chain-of-thought iterate converges to a Berk--Nash fixed point at geometric rate $\rho^\star$ inside an $\eta$-linear sandwich, with phase boundary $\eta_c\!=\!1/D^\star$. The rate is observable: $\widehat\rho^\star$ from logged trajectories predicts $\TV(p_t,p_K)$ decay to within $\pm 0.07$.

\textbf{CT3, closed-form $\eta$-tempering (\Cref{thm:t3-intervention}).} $\Delta\mathrm{Brier}(\eta^\star)\!=\!(1\!-\!\eta^\star)^{2}V_{\mathrm{post}}\!+\!O((1\!-\!\eta^\star)^{3})$. Calibrated temperature scaling~\citep{guo2017oncalibration} acquires a safe-Bayes derivation~\citep{bissiri2016,grunwald2017} that identifies when $\eta^\star\!<\!1$ is the right call.

Extensions in App.~\ref{app:thm-map}: $K$-hop multiplicative composition (\Cref{thm:t15-compositional}) and causal doubly-robust identification of $w^\star$ (\Cref{thm:t16-causal}).

Arbitration was the last hand-tuned step in retrieval-augmented LLM pipelines, and a single functional now gives the optimal combiner, the trajectory envelope, and the closed-form deployment scalar in one place. Six published decoders collapse to special cases on the exponent axis of that functional, and the two interventions ship as one inference-time line over a standard RAG decoder. On the worst conflict cell observed (RLCR-trained $8$B), $\eta$-tempering raises accuracy from $6.3\%$ to $53.1\%$ and Brier from $0.886$ to $0.415$ without a gradient step. The matched-base GRPO-vs-DPO contrast at $+0.344$ ECE, replicated across five families, is the first causal evidence that a specific post-training recipe induces context-overtrust.
