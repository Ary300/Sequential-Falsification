\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A modern language model that answers a factual question is rarely consulting a
single source. In one forward pass it carries a parametric prior
$p_\theta(y\mid q)$ baked in at training, conditions on a retrieved or
supplied context $p_{\ctx}(y\mid q,c)$, and---if it produces a chain of
thought---also listens to itself across $K$ reasoning steps before emitting
an answer. Each voice can be wrong, and they often disagree. A 2023 model
that memorized ``Twitter's CEO is Elon Musk'' will see a 2024 article that
says ``Linda Yaccarino.'' A medical assistant will be asked about a drug
whose label has changed since training. A retrieval pipeline will inject a
poisoned passage. The system has to \emph{arbitrate} between sources, and
the rule it uses is a major lever on what the user sees.

Today this lever is set by hand. Production stacks either trust retrieval
unconditionally, trust the parametric model unconditionally, or interpolate
via tuned thresholds and learned controllers~\citep{mallen2023popqa,
asai2024selfrag,jiang2023flare,yan2024crag,wang2024astuterag}. Decoder-level
methods such as Context-Aware Decoding~\citep{shi2024cad} and its adaptive
variants AdaCAD~\citep{wang2025adacad} and CoCoA~\citep{khandelwal2025cocoa}
are sharper, but none is derived from a posterior over how reliable the
context actually is. Surveys~\citep{xu2024knowledgeconflicts} note that
fixed trust in either source is undesirable, but stop short of a normative
statement of what the right thing would be. In parallel, the calibration
literature on reasoning models has split into two camps that look
contradictory: longer chain-of-thought makes models \emph{better}
calibrated on standard QA~\citep{yoon2025confidence}, but \emph{worse} on
harder or misspecified
tasks~\citep{halawi2024overthinking,lacombe2025dontthinktwice,welch2026cost,
damani2025rlcr,sun2025context,ghosal2025mirage}.

The cost is not abstract. The arbitration rule silently fixes
hallucination rate, calibration when sources \emph{disagree}, and
worst-case behaviour under poisoned or stale retrieval; the same rule
fires on every query in a deployed RAG stack.  The contradictory
calibration findings above are downstream of this gap---each camp is
right about \emph{its} regime, but no theory tells the practitioner
which regime they are in.  RLVR reasoners face the same problem at the
inference layer: recent over-compression
findings~\citep{damani2025rlcr,welch2026cost,ghosal2025mirage} have no
mechanism that ties failure to an actionable quantity.

\paragraph{Our framing.}
We treat knowledge arbitration as a \emph{posterior-predictive decision
problem} and chain-of-thought as endogenous Bayesian evidence. This single
move turns the disagreement between sources, the failure of fixed policies,
and the calibration tension above into three clean theorems. We introduce a
latent context-reliability variable $r\in[0,1]$ with prior $\pi(r)$ and
likelihood $p(c\mid y,r)$; risk is evaluated under any strictly proper
scoring rule (we focus on log loss and Brier).
The mathematical ingredients are classical; the contribution here is to
assemble them into a coherent account of LLM arbitration and to test the
resulting claims on a shared empirical matrix.

\paragraph{Three theorems.}
\textbf{(T1)} Within the log-linear family, the optimal approximation
to the risk-minimizing predictive distribution is the
reliability-weighted geometric mixture
$\hat p_{w^\star}\!\propto\!p_\theta^{1-w^\star}p_{\ctx}^{w^\star}$ with
$w^\star\!=\!\E[r\mid q,c]$ (exact when $\pi(r\mid q,c)$ is concentrated,
$O(\sigma_r^2)$-close otherwise); a plug-in $\hat w$ from any signal
$s(q,c)$ correlated with $r$ achieves excess Bayes risk
$\lesssim\|\hat w-w^\star\|_{L^2}\|p_\theta-p_{\ctx}\|_1$. CAD, AdaCAD,
and CoCoA are special cases or first-order Laplace approximations
(\Cref{cor:special-cases}), with CoCoA requiring extra assumptions to be
consistent for $w^\star$.
\textbf{(T2)} Every constant arbitration weight $w_0$ admits a
sub-family on which it incurs constant excess risk (interior $w_0$ via
a two-family Yao construction; boundary $w_0\!\in\!\{0,1\}$ via the
opposite single sub-family), while a signal-adaptive plug-in attains
$O(n^{-1/2})$ on every distribution.  An optional binary testing
restatement in the appendix makes the same bottleneck explicit.
\textbf{(T3)} Modeling chain-of-thought as $\eta$-generalized Bayes with
endogenous evidence~\citep{bissiri2016,esponda2016,fudenberg2021},
conflict can increase the KL projection distance from truth and therefore
shrink the Gr\"unwald--van Ommen safe rate
$\bar\eta(q,c)$~\citep{grunwald2017,kleijn2006}. When the effective
update rate crosses that smaller threshold, the calibration trajectory
enters the saturation regime we observe empirically on the stable-answer
subset; outside that subset the theorem is descriptive only through the
empirical audits.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{T1 (Bayes-optimal arbitration; \Cref{thm:bayes-arbitration}).}
The Bayes action under any strictly proper score is the
reliability-weighted geometric mixture
$\hat p_{w^\star}\!\propto\!p_\theta^{1-w^\star}\,p_{\ctx}^{w^\star}$
with $w^\star\!=\!\E[r\mid q,c]$.  CAD, AdaCAD, CoCoA, Astute~RAG,
Self-RAG, and JuICE fall out as named special cases
(\Cref{cor:special-cases}); a matched Le~Cam two-point lower bound
(\Cref{thm:lecam-lower}) shows the implied $1/\sqrt n$ plug-in rate
cannot be improved.
\item \textbf{T2 (minimax suboptimality of fixed trust;
\Cref{thm:minimax}).}  Any fixed $w_0\!\in\![0,1]$ is provably
suboptimal across conflict regimes; the construction is tightened
on ConflictBank with explicit $\Delta_+,\Delta_-$
(App.~\ref{app:thm2-yao}) so the bound is non-vacuous.
\item \textbf{T3 (RLVR safe-rate shrinks under conflict;
\Cref{thm:eta-bayes}).}  Long chain-of-thought is an endogenous
generalized-Bayes update; under three audited assumptions the safe
rate $\bar\eta$ shrinks like $c/D^\star$ with $c$ explicit on
finite $\Y$ (App.~\ref{app:thm3-shrinks-explicit}).  Pathwise
tracking of the expected operator is \emph{proved} for the
temperature-$0$ greedy decoding used in our experiments
(\Cref{thm:tracking-tau0}); the stochastic case ($\tau\!>\!0$) is
empirically verified.
\item \textbf{Two inference-time interventions, no retraining.}
A plug-in geometric mixture closes a $+0.083$ regret gap on the
spotlight matrix ($23/25$ cells, e-value $2806$) and beats CoCoA /
Astute~RAG / Self-RAG by $+0.043 / +0.032 / +0.022$ on a powered
$n\!=\!210$ design.  Tempering the post-trace distribution by a
single $\eta^\star$ reduces Brier from $0.903$ to $0.505$ and lifts
accuracy from $0.037$ to $0.440$ on the cell T3 marks as worst.
\item \textbf{Causal evidence and pre-registration.}  A matched
DPO-vs-GRPO contrast at 8B (Llama-3.1-8B base) yields a $+0.344$
ECE gap (causal anchor for T3); three pre-registered held-out
predictions are reported with outcomes (P1/P2 replicate, P3
partial; \Cref{sec:discussion}, App.~\ref{app:preregistered-heldout}).
\end{itemize}
