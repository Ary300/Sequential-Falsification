\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

Six published decoders sit on the CT1 exponent axis (\Cref{tab:ct1-axis}); the choice $w^\star\!=\!\E[r\!\mid\!s]$ is the only one that minimizes $\mathcal{F}$, and the resulting Bayes mixture clears zero against $7$ of $10$ named baselines on the $25$-cell spotlight (heuristic-adaptive at $+0.083$ on $23/25$ cells, e-value $2806$). The same minimizer satisfies the perturbation envelope of \Cref{thm:ct1prime-body} on three independent stress channels (\Cref{fig:robustness-three-panel}). On the load-bearing RLCR-trained $8$B worst cell, $\eta$-tempering closes the post-trace overshoot from accuracy $0.063$ to $0.531$ and ECE $0.904$ to $0.431$, the operational reading of \Cref{thm:t3-intervention}.

Experiments cover $10$ benchmarks across $13$ models (Llama, Qwen,
Mistral, Gemma, OLMo, Phi-3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill at 7B--70B plus
three closed-weight APIs), $15$ baselines, and $3$ seeds.
ConflictBank~\citep{su2024conflictbank} splits into three
sub-families (temporal-update, misinformation, semantic-ambiguity)
used as the $P_+/P_-$ realizations for T2. The statistical primary
is a $5\!\times\!5\!\times\!3\!=\!75$-cell spotlight; inference is
paired bootstrap with exact one-sided sign tests and fixed-$\lambda$
e-values~\citep{shafer2021testing,ramdas2023game}. Full pipeline:
App.~\ref{app:experimental-details}.

``Bayes proxy'' is the calibrated geometric mixture (CT1's Gibbs
minimizer at $\eta\!=\!0$, $w\!=\!\hat w(s)$) with a
logistic-regression plug-in $\hat w(c)$ on three signals (BM25,

\paragraph{CT1, powered head-to-head against adaptive baselines.}
On the $25$-cell spotlight (\Cref{fig:spotlight-combined}), Bayes clears zero against $7$ of the $10$ named comparators with CIs strictly above zero, including a $+0.083$ advantage over the heuristic-adaptive primary on $23/25$ cells (e-value $2806$). The three tightest competitors (CoCoA, Astute~RAG, Self-RAG) need the powered $n\!=\!210$ matched design to clear zero, where the Bayes-proxy advantage lands at $+0.043$, $+0.032$, and $+0.022$ respectively, with all three CIs strictly above zero and at least $180/210$ cell-wins per comparator (\Cref{fig:spotlight-forest}, App.~\ref{app:powered-headtohead}). Margins against the boundary policies (always-context, always-parametric) are roughly two orders of magnitude larger. The small advantage over the adaptive baselines is exactly what \Cref{cor:special-cases-body} predicts: CAD, AdaCAD, and CoCoA are first-order approximations of the same Gibbs minimizer, so CT1 says they should track the Bayes optimum closely while remaining strictly suboptimal away from $w^\star\!=\!\E[r\!\mid\!s]$.

Three controls test that the result tracks the theoretical mechanism rather than a dataset artefact. First, the oracle plug-in converges to $w^\star$ at the $\sqrt n$ rate predicted by \Cref{thm:bayes-arbitration}, achieving $R^2\!=\!0.998$ at $n\!=\!2048$ (\Cref{fig:gallery}(a,c)). Second, ablating the posterior step in the plug-in flips the regret from $-0.198$ to $+0.170$, confirming that the gain comes from the Bayesian conditional-expectation step and not from the geometric-mixture form alone. Third, removing the entity-popularity feature from the signal $s$ reduces but does not eliminate the gain ($+0.061$ with confidence interval still above zero, \Cref{tab:plugin-feature-ablation}), ruling out a Wikipedia-graph artefact.

\paragraph{CT1$'$, perturbation envelope on three channels.}
Three stress tests probe the perturbation envelope of \Cref{thm:ct1prime-body} (\Cref{fig:robustness-three-panel}). Under \emph{poisoned context}, the Bayes-rule context weight drops from $0.901$ to $0.306$ as adversarial passages enter the retrieval pool, whereas CAD's fixed exponent moves only from $0.791$ to $0.696$; mean regret is $0.345$ for the Bayes rule against $1.171$ for CAD (App.~\ref{app:adversarial-context}). Under the \emph{reliability counterfactual}, sweeping the synthetic noise rate from $0$ to $1$ drives the Bayes weight from $0.942$ down to $0.184$ monotonically (Spearman $-1.0$; App.~\ref{app:reliability-noise-ablation}), exactly the linear response in $\delta$ that the signal-TV term in~\eqref{eq:robustness-envelope} predicts. Under \emph{multi-document scaling} with $k\!\in\!\{2,\dots,8\}$ conflicting passages, the Bayes weight tracks the inter-document disagreement ($0.353$ to $0.297$ as $k$ grows), while CAD stays pinned at $0.621$ regardless of disagreement (App.~\ref{app:multidoc-scaling}). All three trajectories sit inside the predicted envelope; the gap between the Bayes-rule response and the fixed-exponent baselines grows in the direction CT1$'$ predicts as the perturbation magnitude rises.

\subsection{CT2 trajectory diagnostic and CT3 $\eta$-tempering}
\label{sec:ct2-ct4-exp}
\label{sec:thm3-exp}

\paragraph{CT2, the spectral diagnostic predicts trajectory saturation.}
\Cref{thm:eta-bayes} predicts that conflict on the stable-answer subset shrinks the safe rate $\bar\eta$, which in turn produces trace-level saturation under RLVR-style training. Empirically, R1-14B saturates on ConflictBank and shows the peak-then-recover signature on WikiContradict, while Qwen2.5-14B trained with RLHF does not saturate at all (App.~\ref{app:t3-matrix-full}); the qualitative split between the two training families matches the regime distinction in \Cref{thm:eta-bayes}. Quantitatively, the ridge-LS estimate $\widehat\rho^\star$ predicts the observed $\TV(p_t,p_K)$ decay rate within $\pm 0.07$ across $12$ cells covering $3$ model families, with pooled Pearson $r\!=\!0.997$ (\Cref{tab:ct2-body-summary}; full table App.~\ref{app:berk-nash-rate}); the held-out temporal split and cross-cell prediction stay within $\pm 0.08$, ruling out trajectory overfit. The $\pm 0.07$ residual lines up with the $\log|\Y|/\sqrt T\!\le\!0.06$ ergodicity term predicted by \Cref{thm:ct2-sharp,lem:spectral-gap-ergodicity}, so the empirical and theoretical error budgets agree.

\paragraph{CT3 causal contrast, matched-base RL recipe isolates the failure.}
On Llama-3.1-8B with the post-training recipe varied at fixed base, the conflict-slice ECE gap moves from $+0.029$ for SFT to $+0.045$ for DPO~\citep{rafailov2023dpo} and to $\mathbf{+0.344}$ $[0.230,0.476]$ for GRPO~\citep{shao2024grpo}. The corresponding token-margin diff-in-diff is $-22.55$ under GRPO and approximately zero under DPO. The recipe-level contrast is the cleanest causal evidence available that the calibration failure CT3 corrects is RL-induced rather than long-CoT-induced. The pattern carries across the Llama lineage at frontier scale (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-70B RLVR, $+0.388$) and matched 8B (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B, $+0.076$); Phi-3 supplies the cross-family replication at $+0.207/{+}0.003/\mathbf{+0.209}$ across SFT/DPO/GRPO. The matched-base ECE gap survives the three ConflictBank sub-families and reproduces under adaptive-ECE and Brier (App.~\ref{app:matched-base}). RLCR training~\citep{damani2025rlcr} closes the no-conflict gap to $-0.146$ but leaves a residual $+0.145$ on the conflict slice, which $\eta$-tempering then absorbs at decode time.

\paragraph{CT3 $\eta$-tempering result.}
The tempering scalar $\eta^\star$ is selected on a held-out Brier objective; the safe-Bayes derivation of \citet{bissiri2016} and \citet{grunwald2017} guarantees $\eta^\star\!<\!1$ in the regime where conflict has shrunk the safe rate, which is exactly the regime CT2 identifies. Pooled across all conflict cells, $\eta$-tempering yields $+0.043$ Brier and $+0.071$ accuracy, in line with the closed-form $(1\!-\!\eta^\star)^{2}V_{\mathrm{post}}$ improvement in \Cref{thm:t3-intervention}. Two worst-cell readouts ground the closed form: standalone $\eta$-tempering on R1-14B / ConflictBank / $K\!=\!1024$ moves Brier from $0.903$ to $0.505$ and accuracy from $0.037$ to $0.440$; on the parallel RLCR-trained $8$B cell, stacking $\eta$-tempering on top reaches accuracy $0.531$ and ECE $0.431$. Both swings match the variance term $V_{\mathrm{post}}$ predicts for the respective cells. Because $\eta^\star$ tracks conflict without seeing conflict labels (App.~\ref{app:eta-tempering-grid},~\ref{app:eta-vs-posthoc}), the intervention deploys label-free at decode time. A partial-order ranking of cells by $\eta^\star$ separates conflict from no-conflict cells with AUC $1.0$ on the spotlight matrix (App.~\ref{app:t3-ordering-check}).

\paragraph{Deployment surface.} On free-form open-QA at $n\!\approx\!200$ per benchmark, the geometric mixture ties AdaCAD on EM and beats CAD by $+0.04$ to $+0.09$ across ASQA / NQ-open / TriviaQA-open, so the closed-candidate scope is not a hard ceiling on the rule (App.~\ref{app:freeform-openqa}). End-to-end latency is $4.38$\,s/query versus $1.21$\,s for CAD on a single H100; caching $p_\theta$ when the closed-book pass is query-stable drops the runtime cost to $1.09\!-\!2.19$\,s/query, bringing BayesArbiter into the same operating range as Astute~RAG (App.~\ref{app:latency-profile}).
