\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

Experiments are organized around the three theorem claims, evaluated
on a shared matrix of ten benchmarks, thirteen models (Llama / Qwen /
Mistral / Gemma / OLMo / Phi-3 / DeepSeek-R1-Distill at 7B--70B,
plus three closed-weight APIs), fifteen baselines, and three seeds.
ConflictBank~\citep{su2024conflictbank}, the load-bearing conflict
benchmark, decomposes into three sub-families (temporal-update,
misinformation, semantic-ambiguity) used as the $P_+/P_-$
realizations for T2.  The statistical primary is the spotlight
matrix ($5\!\times\!5\!\times\!3\!=\!75$ cells); cells (not rows)
are the unit of paired-bootstrap inference, and all main claims use
paired bootstrap, exact one-sided sign tests, and fixed-$\lambda$
e-values~\citep{shafer2021testing,ramdas2023game}.  Full pipeline
in \Cref{fig:exp-arch}, App.~\ref{app:experimental-details}.

\subsection{T1 \& T2: arbitration and fixed-policy failure}
\label{sec:thm12-exp}

``Bayes proxy'' is the calibrated geometric-mixture rule with a
logistic-regression plug-in $\hat w(c)$ on three signals (BM25,
popularity, semantic entropy); a fully Bayesian Laplace variant is
indistinguishable (App.~\ref{app:method-choices}).  Across a
\emph{broad} and a \emph{conflict-heavy} wave, the best-tuned
fixed-trust competitor (held-out tuning) trails by ${\sim}2{\times}$
in regret (\Cref{tab:headline-regret}; broad $-0.046$ vs.\ $-0.023$,
conflict-heavy $-0.126$ vs.\ $-0.075$).  The boundary policies
collapse by two orders of magnitude on whichever wave disagrees with
their default---the asymmetric cost of a wrong fixed trust that T2
predicts.

\paragraph{Does it beat published adaptive methods?}
\Cref{fig:spotlight-combined} answers cell by cell: the Bayes proxy
(mean regret $-0.172$) leads every named comparator, with the
strongest heuristic the load-bearing anchor ($+0.083$, CI
$[0.037,0.111]$, $23/25$ wins, $e\!=\!2806$).  Methods closer in form
to the geometric mixture trail by smaller margins than ones with more
aggressive context trust---numerical ordering matches T1's
special-cases corollary.

The visual panel of \Cref{fig:spotlight-combined} confirms the
ordering: methods losing hardest are looser approximations of the
same geometric form, while boundary policies fail asymmetrically on
the wave they disagree with---the failure mode T2 predicts.

Since CoCoA's 25-cell CI is closest to zero, we ran a larger matched
design ($n\!=\!210$) against CoCoA, Astute~RAG, and Self-RAG: powered
intervals all land strictly above zero ($+0.043,+0.032,+0.022$;
$\geq\!180/210$ cell wins; \Cref{fig:spotlight-forest},

rare entities, not the rule; App.~\ref{app:per-bench-wins}).

(\Cref{fig:per-model} left; Llama-3.1-70B at $+0.060$); the frontier

Two sanity checks: on a controlled oracle the plug-in tracks
$w^\star$ at $\sqrt n$ ($R^2\!=\!0.998$ at $n\!=\!2048$); violating
log-linearity degrades smoothly ($R^2\!\to\!0.961/0.729$;
\Cref{fig:gallery}(a),(c)).  Ablating the posterior step flips
$-0.198\!\to\!+0.170$---the gain isn't a tuned constant.

\paragraph{Takeaway (T1 \& T2).}
The geometric mixture beats every named baseline; any fixed trust
value collapses on the wave it's wrong about.

\subsection{T3: real reasoning traces and the two-regime law}
\label{sec:thm3-exp}

T3 has two coupled claims.  The load-bearing one---pre-registered
and replicated (App.~\ref{app:preregistered-heldout})---is that
conflict shrinks $\bar\eta(q,c)$ and $\eta$-tempering closes the
overconfidence on the marked cells.  The narrower binary
saturation-vs-recovery classifier does not replicate cleanly on every
held-out family (P3) even as the safe-rate ranking does.
$\hat\eta_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is a calibrated proxy
(App.~\ref{app:eta-proxy-id}): rank transfers, magnitude recalibrates
per benchmark.

The trajectory matrix (App.~\ref{app:t3-matrix-full}) shows the
within-model benchmark dependence T3 predicts: R1-14B saturates on
ConflictBank ($0.59\!\to\!0.95\!\to\!0.95$) and peaks-then-recovers
on WikiContradict ($0.27\!\to\!0.45\!\to\!0.38$); Qwen2.5-14B (RLHF)
does not saturate on either.

\Cref{fig:matched-base-evidence} reports the matched-base evidence.
Holding Llama-3.1-8B fixed and varying only the post-training recipe:
SFT (control for ``extra training'') sits at $+0.029$ gap (CI
$[-0.058,0.115]$, statistically ambiguous), DPO at $+0.045$, GRPO at
$+0.344$ (CI $[0.230,0.476]$).  The GRPO$-$SFT separation is
$+0.315$---extra supervised tokens alone do not produce the conflict
gap, only the RL recipe does.  Phi-3 GRPO replicates the direction
on a second family at $+0.209$ gap (cross-family RL replication
row); the GRPO blowup also survives all three ConflictBank
sub-families separately (misinformation $+0.572$, temporal $+0.690$,
semantic $+0.629$; \Cref{tab:grpo-subfamily-survival}) and
transfers across ECE / adaptive ECE / Brier
(\Cref{tab:metric-robustness}).  Nine cross-family/scale controls
(Mistral, two Gemma sizes, Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct, OLMo-2 DPO/GRPO,
Qwen-14B DPO/GRPO) all sit below $0.07$ in absolute gap, ruling out
both the long-CoT confound and ``scale alone explains it''; the right panel shows the
same training-type signature at the trace level.  RLCR (strongest mitigation) closes no-conflict
($-0.146$) but leaves conflict degraded ($+0.145$, gap $+0.291$);
inference-time $\eta$-tempering absorbs the residual
(\Cref{tab:rlcr-joint-pilot}, App.~\ref{app:reviewer-qa}).  Qwen
replicates direction at smaller magnitude because Qwen2.5-Instruct is
pre-saturated on ConflictBank, leaving the matched contrast with
little headroom.

Trace diagnostics confirm the sharpness $\eta$-tempering exploits
(preferred beats runner-up on $0.81$ of wrong-answer cells, median
$8.5$ logits; full trajectories in App.~\ref{app:calibration-diagrams}).

\paragraph{Implied intervention: $\eta$-tempered decoding.}
We pick $\eta^\star$ by held-out Brier on a $200$-sample calibration
fold and report on a disjoint $300$-sample evaluation fold (leakage
audit, App.~\ref{app:eta-leakage-audit}).  On the worst slice
$\eta^\star$ hits the boundary, dropping Brier $0.903\!\to\!0.505$
and accuracy $0.037\!\to\!0.440$ (\Cref{fig:eta-tempering} left;
\Cref{tab:eta-per-slice}).  Selected
temperatures track conflict without being told: conflict cells
prefer smaller $\eta^\star$ ($0.325$ vs.\ $0.625$, $0.52\times$;
replicates on R1-7B/32B).  The mechanism is distinct from post-hoc
calibration: Platt/isotonic match Brier ($0.017,0.016$) but leave
accuracy fixed (post-argmax); $\eta$-tempering moves both
(pre-argmax, App.~\ref{app:eta-vs-posthoc}).

\textit{Takeaway (T3).}  RLVR overconfidence is real,
training-type-causal (matched SFT/DPO/GRPO + Phi-3 GRPO replication),
robust across metrics and ConflictBank sub-families, and corrected by
a single inference-time knob ($\eta^\star$ from held-out Brier)---no
retraining needed.  The partial-order ranking version of T3's
saturation prediction replicates with pairwise AUC $1.0$ on the
held-out cells (App.~\ref{app:t3-ordering-check}).
