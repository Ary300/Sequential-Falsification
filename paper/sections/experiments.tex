\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

Three theorems, three things to falsify. CT1 says one specific arbitration rule is optimal and the rest of the literature lives on its exponent axis. CT2 says the chain-of-thought decay rate is something you can read off a logged trajectory. CT3 says the readout-time fix has a closed-form payoff. We put each one in front of every comparator we could find and watch where it bends. The headline: \textsc{BayesArbiter} is the only Gibbs minimizer of the underlying free energy, and the six published decoders that sit alongside it on that axis (\Cref{tab:ct1-axis}) lose by margins the theory predicts. On the worst cell we observed, an RLCR-trained 8B that flips into post-trace collapse, $\eta$-tempering pulls accuracy from $6.3\%$ back to $53.1\%$ without retraining the model.

What we ran, in one breath: $12$ conflict and open-QA benchmarks, $13$ model checkpoints from 7B to 70B across seven families plus three frontier APIs, $15$ baselines, $3$ seeds. The CT1/CT2 spotlight is a $5\!\times\!5\!\times\!4$ matrix; CT3 runs a five-benchmark size-scaling sweep; the matched-base contrast is a shared LoRA inventory with per-family overrides. Inference for everyone: paired bootstrap ($10{,}000$ resamples, seed $42$), one-sided sign tests, fixed-$\lambda$ e-values~\citep{shafer2021testing,ramdas2023game}. The full inventory (per-matrix configurations, training defaults, family overrides, evaluation runner, compute footprint) lives in App.~\ref{app:exp-design-inventory}; prompts and decoding hyperparameters in App.~\ref{app:experimental-details}.

Three mechanism controls. The oracle plug-in converges to $w^\star$ at the $\sqrt n$ rate of \Cref{thm:bayes-arbitration} ($R^2\!=\!0.998$ at $n\!=\!2048$, \Cref{fig:gallery}(a,c)). Ablating the posterior step in the plug-in flips regret $-0.198\!\to\!+0.170$, isolating the Bayesian conditional-expectation step from the geometric-mixture form. Removing the popularity signal reduces the gain to $+0.061$ (CI still above zero, \Cref{tab:plugin-feature-ablation}), ruling out a Wikipedia-graph artefact.

Three stress channels probe \Cref{thm:ct1prime-body} (\Cref{fig:robustness-three-panel}). \emph{Poisoned context}: \textsc{BayesArbiter} context weight drops $0.901\!\to\!0.306$ as adversarial passages enter; CAD's fixed exponent moves only $0.791\!\to\!0.696$; mean regret $0.345$ vs.\ $1.171$ for CAD (App.~\ref{app:adversarial-context}). \emph{Reliability counterfactual}: sweeping the noise rate $0\!\to\!1$ drives the weight $0.942\!\to\!0.184$ monotonically (Spearman $-1.0$), the linear response in $\delta$ predicted by~\eqref{eq:robustness-envelope}. \emph{Multi-document scaling}: $k\!\in\!\{2,\dots,8\}$ tracks inter-document disagreement ($0.353\!\to\!0.297$) while CAD stays pinned at $0.621$ (App.~\ref{app:multidoc-scaling}). All three sit inside the envelope.

\subsection{CT2 trajectory diagnostic and CT3 $\eta$-tempering}
\label{sec:ct2-ct4-exp}
\label{sec:thm3-exp}

For CT2, \Cref{thm:eta-bayes} predicts that conflict on the stable-answer subset shrinks the safe rate $\bar\eta$, producing trace-level saturation under RLVR. R1-14B saturates on ConflictBank and shows the peak-then-recover signature on WikiContradict; Qwen2.5-14B (RLHF) does not saturate (App.~\ref{app:t3-matrix-full}). Quantitatively, $\widehat\rho^\star$ predicts $\TV(p_t,p_K)$ decay within $\pm 0.07$ across $12$ cells / $3$ families (pooled $r\!=\!0.997$, \Cref{tab:ct2-body-summary}); held-out temporal split and cross-cell prediction stay within $\pm 0.08$. The $\pm 0.07$ residual matches the $\log|\Y|/\sqrt T\!\le\!0.06$ ergodicity term of \Cref{thm:ct2-sharp,lem:spectral-gap-ergodicity}.

For CT3, the matched-base RL contrast isolates the failure mode at the recipe level. On Llama-3.1-8B with the post-training recipe varied at fixed base, conflict-slice ECE gap moves $+0.029$ (SFT) $\to$ $+0.045$ (DPO~\citep{rafailov2023dpo}) $\to$ $\mathbf{+0.344}$ $[0.230,0.476]$ (GRPO~\citep{shao2024grpo}); token-margin diff-in-diff is $-22.55$ under GRPO and $\approx\!0$ under DPO. The pattern replicates on the Llama lineage at frontier scale (DeepSeek-Llama-70B RLVR, $+0.388$); the distilled DeepSeek-Llama-8B at $+0.053$ rules out distillation alone. Phi-3 supplies cross-family replication ($+0.207/+0.003/\mathbf{+0.209}$ over SFT/DPO/GRPO); Mistral-7B ($+0.164$) and Gemma-2-9B ($+0.103$) SFT add two more families in the same direction (App.~\ref{app:matched-base}). RLCR~\citep{damani2025rlcr} closes the no-conflict gap to $-0.146$ but leaves a $+0.145$ residual on the conflict slice, which $\eta$-tempering absorbs at decode time.

The $\eta$-tempering scalar $\eta^\star$ is selected on held-out Brier; the safe-Bayes derivation~\citep{bissiri2016,grunwald2017} guarantees $\eta^\star\!<\!1$ in the conflict-shrunk-safe-rate regime CT2 identifies. Pooled across conflict cells, $\eta$-tempering yields $+0.043$ Brier and $+0.071$ accuracy, matching $(1\!-\!\eta^\star)^{2}V_{\mathrm{post}}$. Worst-cell readouts: standalone $\eta$-tempering on R1-14B / ConflictBank / $K\!=\!1024$ moves Brier $0.903\!\to\!0.505$ and accuracy $0.037\!\to\!0.440$; on the RLCR-trained $8$B cell, stacking $\eta$-tempering reaches accuracy $0.531$, ECE $0.431$, Brier $0.415$ (from $0.886$). $\eta^\star$ tracks conflict label-free (App.~\ref{app:eta-tempering-grid},~\ref{app:eta-vs-posthoc}); ranking cells by $\eta^\star$ separates conflict from no-conflict at AUC $1.0$ (App.~\ref{app:t3-ordering-check}).

On the deployment side, free-form open-QA at $n\!\approx\!200$/benchmark shows that the mixture ties AdaCAD on EM and beats CAD by $+0.04$ to $+0.09$ on ASQA/NQ-open/TriviaQA-open (App.~\ref{app:freeform-openqa}). End-to-end latency: $4.38$\,s/query vs.\ $1.21$\,s for CAD on a single H100; caching $p_\theta$ when the closed-book pass is query-stable drops cost to $1.09$--$2.19$\,s/query, the Astute~RAG range (App.~\ref{app:latency-profile}).
