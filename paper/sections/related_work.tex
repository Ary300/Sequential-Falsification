\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Knowledge conflict benchmarks and analyses.}
Recent work has mapped the space of knowledge conflicts in LLMs into
context-memory, inter-context, and intra-memory forms
\citep{xu2024knowledgeconflicts}. ConflictQA and related studies show that
models are highly sensitive to coherent counter-memory and can exhibit
confirmation bias under multi-evidence conflict \citep{xie2024conflictqa}.
Large benchmark efforts such as ConflictBank, WikiContradict, and DynamicQA
provide the empirical substrate for studying controlled and naturalistic
conflicts \citep{su2024conflictbank,hou2024wikicontradict,marjanovic2024dynamicqa}.
These papers give the task surface, but not a Bayes-optimal or minimax account
of how arbitration should work.

\paragraph{Adaptive retrieval is mostly heuristic.}
PopQA-style adaptive retrieval uses popularity as a practical signal for when
retrieval helps, but the resulting policy is threshold-based rather than
decision-theoretically optimal \citep{mallen2023popqa}. Self-RAG and related
adaptive-RAG systems similarly learn or tune retrieval decisions without
deriving them from a posterior-predictive optimum \citep{asai2024selfrag}. Our
Theorems 1 and 2 aim at exactly this gap: a reliability-aware arbitration rule
and a formal statement that fixed trust policies are structurally dominated on
conflict families.

\paragraph{Mechanistic and calibration views of overthinking.}
Recent work shows that longer reasoning can hurt accuracy or calibration.
``Overthinking the Truth'' identifies a depth-wise failure mode in which models
move from correct intermediate beliefs to incorrect final ones under misleading
context \citep{halawi2024overthinking}. Chen et al.\ show that training-time
cross-entropy sharpening can harm pass@$N$, while Ghosal et al.\ document
non-monotone accuracy under large reasoning budgets \citep{chen2025rethinking,
ghosal2025mirage}. Sun et al.\ report that rationales and context reiteration
increase context reliance, especially on tasks where parametric knowledge
matters \citep{sun2025context}. Our calibration result is adjacent to this
literature but differs in two ways: it is conditioned on knowledge conflict,
and it is framed as a posterior misspecification phenomenon rather than a
purely empirical observation.

\paragraph{Misspecified Bayes as a template.}
The theorem-3 mechanism is best understood through classical results on
misspecified Bayesian updating. Berk established that under misspecification the
posterior concentrates on a KL projection rather than the truth
\citep{berk1966}. Later work shows that misspecified posteriors can be
arbitrarily overconfident and that bagging or other robustification can reduce
that pathology \citep{grunwald2017,huggins2020bayesbag}. We use this literature
as the conceptual bridge between reasoning-time source conflict and the
confidence blow-up seen in real reasoning traces.
