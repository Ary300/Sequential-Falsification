\section{Setup and Theory}
\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{Setup.}
A query $q$ together with retrieved context $c$ has a true answer $y^\star$
drawn from $\PP^\star$. The LLM exposes the closed-book softmax
$p_\theta(y\mid q)$ and the context-conditioned softmax
$p_{\ctx}(y\mid q,c)$. Latent reliability
$r:=\PP(y^\star=y_{\ctx}\mid y_{\ctx}\!\neq\! y_\param,q,c)\in[0,1]$ has
prior $\pi(r)$ informed by observable signals $s(q,c)$ (retrieval/BM25
score, popularity, semantic entropy~\citep{kuhn2023semantic}, contradiction
type). The decision maker chooses $\hat p\!\in\!\Delta(\Y)$; risk is
$R(\hat p)=\E_{q,c,y^\star}[S(\hat p,y^\star)]$ for a strictly proper
scoring rule $S$ (we use log loss and Brier). We assume
$y^\star\!\perp\!c\mid (q,r)$ (Assumption~\ref{app:assumption-ci}) and a
log-linear conditional answer model
(\Cref{app:assumption-loglinear}).

\subsection{Bayes-optimal arbitration (T1)}
\label{sec:thm1}

\begin{theorem}[Posterior-predictive arbitration within the log-linear family]
\label{thm:bayes-arbitration}
Under the assumptions above, the unique minimizer of $R$ is the
posterior predictive
$p^\star(y\mid q,c)\!=\!\int p(y\mid q,c,r)\pi(r\mid q,c)\,dr$.  Within
the log-linear family, the information-geometric projection
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\hat p_{w^\star}(y\mid q,c)\;\propto\;p_\theta(y\mid q)^{1-w^\star(q,c)}\,
p_{\ctx}(y\mid q,c)^{w^\star(q,c)},\qquad
w^\star(q,c)=\E[r\mid q,c]
\;}
\label{eq:thm1}
\end{equation}
is the unique minimizer of
$(1-w)\KL(p\|p_\theta)+w\KL(p\|p_{\ctx})$ at
$w=w^\star(q,c)$ (\Cref{prop:thm1-variational}).
It is exact when $\pi(r\mid q,c)$ is degenerate, and in general
$\KL(p^\star\Vert\hat p_{w^\star})=
\tfrac12\sigma_r^2(q,c)\,V(q,c)+O(\sigma_r^4)$,
where $\sigma_r^2(q,c)\!=\!\Var(r\mid q,c)$ and $V(q,c)$ is a
local log-likelihood curvature.  For any plug-in
$\hat w(q,c)$ on $n$ calibration points, the induced excess risk is
controlled by the weight-estimation error: under $0/1$ loss,
\[
R_{0/1}(\hat y_{\hat w})-R_{0/1}(\hat y_{w^\star})
\;\leq\;
\|\hat w-w^\star\|_{L^2}\,\|p_\theta-p_{\ctx}\|_1,
\]
and under log loss it is quadratic in $\|\hat w-w^\star\|_{L^2}$
(\Cref{lem:excess-risk-log,lem:excess-risk-zero-one}).  A
$\sqrt n$-consistent plug-in yields $O(n^{-1/2})$ excess $0/1$ risk
and $O(n^{-1})$ excess log loss, up to the approximation gap.  This
$1/\sqrt n$ rate is order-optimal: a matched Le~Cam two-point lower
bound (\Cref{thm:lecam-lower}) shows no plug-in estimator of
$w^\star$ can do strictly better in minimax, and the Pinsker-route
$0/1$ constant is tight up to a factor of $2$ (App.~\ref{app:thm1}).
\end{theorem}

The contribution is matching this classical chain to the decoder-side
arbitration problem; the $O(\sigma_r^2)$ Laplace gap is audited on
the spotlight matrix and stays below $10^{-5}$ nat per cell
(App.~\ref{app:t1-variance-correction}).  A corollary recovers
existing decoder-side methods as points on the same axis: CAD is
$w\!=\!2$, AdaCAD is the first-order Laplace approximation of
$\E[r\mid q,c]$, CoCoA a non-Bayesian ensemble of reliability proxies,
and the always-context / always-parametric defaults are the boundaries
$w\!\in\!\{0,1\}$ (\Cref{cor:special-cases}).  A structural
strengthening (\Cref{thm:t1-sufficient}) shows $\E[r\mid s]$ is the
unique Blackwell-sufficient signal statistic, so the geometric
mixture with $\phi^\star(s)\!=\!\E[r\mid s]$ is the unique
risk-minimizer among log-linear policies---explaining why
CAD/AdaCAD/CoCoA empirically converge to the geometric form.  Under
exact closure of the posterior predictive inside that class,
App.~\ref{app:thm1-strengthenings} strengthens this again: the same
weight is the unique minimizer for every strictly proper scoring rule,
and any decoder proxy is optimal if and only if it makes
$\E[r\mid s]$ measurable.  For
free-form generation, the variational characterization extends to a
per-token chain mixture with a quantifiable factorization gap
(\Cref{cor:sequence-extension}); load-bearing assumptions are
properties of the task, not the weights, so closed-weight LLMs
satisfy the same conditions (App.~\ref{app:closed-model-assumptions}).

\subsection{Minimax suboptimality of fixed trust (T2)}
\label{sec:thm2}

Let $\A_{\mathrm{fix}}=\{\hat y_{w_0}: w_0\in[0,1]\}$ be fixed-trust
policies and $\A_{\mathrm{adapt}}(s)$ be signal-reading plug-ins
$\hat y_{\hat w(s)}$.

\begin{theorem}[Fixed trust is not uniformly optimal across conflicting sub-families]
\label{thm:minimax}
Assume there exist two sub-families $P_+$ and $P_-$ and constants
$\Delta_+,\Delta_->0$ such that, for every fixed trust value
$w\in[0,1]$,
\[
R(\hat y_w;P_+)-R(\hat y^\star;P_+) \ge \Delta_+(1-w),
\qquad
R(\hat y_w;P_-)-R(\hat y^\star;P_-) \ge \Delta_- w.
\]
Then no fixed trust policy is uniformly optimal over mixtures of
$P_+$ and $P_-$.  In particular, for any fixed $w_0$ and any mixture
$P_\lambda=\lambda P_+ + (1-\lambda)P_-$,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
R(\hat y_{w_0};P_\lambda)-R(\hat y^\star;P_\lambda)
\;\ge\;
\lambda\Delta_+(1-w_0)+(1-\lambda)\Delta_- w_0
\;}
\label{eq:thm2}
\end{equation}
Hence every fixed $w_0$ incurs constant excess risk on some mixture over
the two sub-families.  By contrast, if a signal-adaptive plug-in
$\hat y_{\hat w(s)}$ identifies the active sub-family with error
$\varepsilon_s$, then
$R(\hat y_{\hat w};P)-R(\hat y^\star;P)\leq C\varepsilon_s+O(n^{-1/2})$.
Without informative signal information, binary testing lower bounds
prevent the minimax excess risk from vanishing on this family.
\end{theorem}

App.~\ref{app:thm2} sharpens this into a signal-separability statement:
if $\varepsilon_s$ is the Bayes error for recovering the active
sub-family from signal $s$, then every $s$-measurable policy pays at
least $(\Delta_{\min}/2)\varepsilon_s$ excess risk under the balanced
mixture, while the adaptive plug-in achieves
$O(\varepsilon_s)+O(n^{-1/2})$.

$\Delta_+,\Delta_-$ summarize how sharply the two sub-families prefer
opposite trust decisions; benign benchmarks can have one small,
explaining why fixed-trust degradation in \Cref{tab:headline-regret}
is benchmark-dependent.  App.~\ref{app:thm2-yao} shows how to
instantiate these constants from annotated sub-family regret slices
when a benchmark exposes that structure, but the theorem itself is
intentionally benchmark-agnostic.

T1 and T2 settle the static question.  Modern systems reason first,
each trace token becoming endogenous evidence; T3 carries the
Bayesian posture into that loop.

\subsection{Chain-of-thought as endogenous generalized Bayes (T3)}
\label{sec:thm3}

A reasoning model produces a trace $z_{1:K}$. Each $z_t$ is sampled
from the model's own distribution conditioned on the prefix and then
conditioned on by the next segment, so the trace is not external
evidence---it is endogenous, and the model is updating on its own
samples. We model the implicit answer distribution after step $t$ as
a generalized Bayes update~\citep{bissiri2016}:
\begin{equation}
p_{t+1}(y\mid q,c)\;\propto\;p_t(y\mid q,c)\,\exp\!\left(-\eta\,\ell_t(y;q,c,z_t)\right),
\qquad
z_t\sim\mu(\cdot\mid p_t,q,c).
\label{eq:gen-bayes}
\end{equation}
Let $\bar\eta(q,c)$ be the Gr\"unwald--van~Ommen safe rate for this
update~\citep{grunwald2017}.  The next proposition makes the
Berk--Nash language a literal statement about the dynamics
of~\eqref{eq:gen-bayes} rather than a structural analogy borrowed
from the misspecification literature.

\begin{proposition}[Reduction of autoregressive gen-Bayes decoding to Berk--Nash dynamics]
\label{prop:berk-nash-reduction}
Let $\Phi_\eta(p)(y)\propto p(y)\,\E_{z\sim\mu(\cdot\mid p,q,c)}\!\exp(-\eta\,\ell(y;q,c,z))$
be the expected update operator.  With bounded loss
$\ell\!\in\![0,L]$ and a Lipschitz sampling kernel,
$\Phi_\eta$ is a continuous self-map of $\Delta(\Y)$ whose fixed
points are exactly the Berk--Nash equilibria of the auxiliary game in
which belief $p$ generates data $\mu(\cdot\mid p,q,c)$
(App.~\ref{app:berk-nash-reduction}).  Any realized trajectory that
tracks $\Phi_\eta$ and converges lands at such a fixed point---a
generalized KL projection $\arg\min_p D_\eta(P^\star\Vert p)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Conflict shrinks the safe rate; overshoot creates saturation]
\label{thm:eta-bayes}
Assume \textup{(i)} $y^\star$ is unreachable
by~\eqref{eq:gen-bayes}~\citep{berk1966,kleijn2006};
\textup{(ii)} $z_t$ is endogenous~\citep{esponda2016,fudenberg2021};
\textup{(iii)} conflict increases the KL projection distance
$D^\star(q,c)$ relative to matched controls
(App.~\ref{app:dstar-per-cell}).  Then
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\bar\eta(q,c)\;\le\;\frac{c_{|\Y|,\pi}}{D^\star(q,c)},\qquad
\eta\!>\!\bar\eta(q,c)\;\Longrightarrow\;G(t)\text{ non-decreasing on the stable-answer subset}
\;}
\label{eq:thm3}
\end{equation}
with $c_{|\Y|,\pi}$ the finite-alphabet entropy/prior constant
(App.~\ref{app:safe-rate-detail}).  High-conflict cells have smaller
$\bar\eta$; above it, \eqref{eq:gen-bayes} hypercompresses around a
KL-projection answer faster than accuracy improves, so the calibration
gap
$G(t)=|\E_{p_t}\one{Y\!=\!y^\star}-\one{\hat y_t\!=\!y^\star}|$
is non-decreasing on the stable-answer subset.
\end{theorem}

The two-regime trajectory (\Cref{cor:two-regime}) is the falsifiable
prediction: peak-then-recover for $\eta\!\lesssim\!\bar\eta$,
saturation otherwise, with transition benchmark-dependent through
$\KL(p_\theta\|p_{\ctx})$.  At temperature $0$ the realized trajectory
equals the expected operator exactly (\Cref{thm:tracking-tau0}), so
the conclusion holds unconditionally on the stable-answer
subset---$8.0\%$ of R1-14B / ConflictBank-conflict cells across
$\{0,128,1024\}$, $36.4\%$ across the post-jump window
$\{128,1024\}$.  App.~\ref{app:thm3} further decomposes the full-sample
gap into stable and flip components:
$\Delta G=\PP(S)\Delta G^{S}+\PP(F)\Delta G^{F}\geq
\PP(S)\Delta G^{S}-\PP(F)$, so the whole-population calibration curve
inherits the stable-subset overshoot up to the flip probability.

\subsection{The directly implied intervention: $\eta$-tempered decoding}
\label{sec:eta-tempering}

If the effective rate is too high, lower it at the readout.  Temper
the post-trace answer distribution by $\eta\in[0,1]$, leaving the
trace untouched:
$p_{\eta}(y\mid\mathrm{trace},q,c)\!\propto\!p(y\mid\mathrm{trace},q,c)^{\eta}$.
We pick $\eta$ on a held-out split by Brier-optimization with a
do-no-harm fallback.  This is not standard temperature scaling---it
operates only on answer tokens, leaving the endogenous-evidence loop
intact (App.~\ref{app:eta-tempering-derivation}).
A theorem-to-anchor map is given in \Cref{tab:thm-map} (App.~\ref{app:thm-map}).
