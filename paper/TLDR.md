# TL;DR

**When Should LLMs Trust Retrieval? Bayes-Optimal Knowledge Arbitration under Conflict**

A retrieval-augmented LLM has three voices in its ear — what it learned during training, what the retrieved document says, and what its own chain of thought concludes — and production systems pick a winner with a hand-tuned rule that silently sets hallucination rate, calibration, and worst-case behavior under poisoned retrieval; we show there's exactly one optimal rule, a reliability-weighted blend whose weight is the model's posterior over how trustworthy the context is, and that six published decoders (CAD, AdaCAD, CoCoA, Astute, Self-RAG, JuICE) all collapse to approximations of this same rule on a single axis, while the same object also predicts when long chain-of-thought helps calibration and when it hurts (settling a debate the field had split into two camps); two inference-time interventions fall out, each one line over a standard RAG decoder with no retraining, and on the worst conflict cell observed — an RLCR-trained 8B in post-trace collapse — the readout-time fix lifts accuracy from 6% to 53% without a single gradient step, while a matched-base contrast across five model families gives the first causal evidence that a specific RL recipe induces context-overtrust — arbitration was the last hand-tuned step in the RAG stack, and it doesn't need to be anymore.
