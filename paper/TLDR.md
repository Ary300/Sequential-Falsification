# TL;DR

**When Should LLMs Trust Retrieval? Bayes-Optimal Knowledge Arbitration under Conflict**

Every retrieval-augmented LLM has three voices in its ear that often disagree: what it learned during training, what the retrieved document says, and what its own chain of thought concludes. Production systems pick a winner with a hand-tuned rule, and that single choice silently sets the system's hallucination rate, its calibration on every conflict slice, and its worst-case behavior when retrieval is poisoned or stale. We show there's exactly one optimal rule, a reliability-weighted blend whose weight is the model's posterior over how trustworthy the context is given the query, and that six published decoders (CAD, AdaCAD, CoCoA, Astute, Self-RAG, JuICE) collapse to approximations of this same rule on a single axis. Two inference-time fixes drop out, each one line over a standard RAG decoder with no retraining, and on the worst conflict cell we observed they lift an 8B model's accuracy from 6 percent to 53 percent without a single gradient step.
